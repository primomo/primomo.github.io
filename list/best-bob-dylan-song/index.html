Bob Dylan was born Robert Allen Zimmerman in the City of Duluth on 24th May 1941 and grow up in the small town of Hibbing, Minnesota. Bob Dylan would become one of the greatest singer songwriters ever, from his folk and Bluegrass roots to his American civil rights anthems to going 'Electric', Dylan has spanned five decades and has influenced many.
Dylan, in his lifetime has won over 40 major awards for his songwriting, these awards include 12 Grammys, a Golden Globe and Academy Award trophies. 2016, saw Bob Dylan be awarded the Nobel prize for literature "for having created new poetic expressions within the great American song tradition".
